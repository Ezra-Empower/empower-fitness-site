The Real Reason Your Injury Keeps Coming Back

By Dr. Ezra Miller, PT, DPT  ·  Empower Fitness PT  ·  Boca Raton, FL

You rest it. It gets better. You go back to playing. It comes back. If this sounds familiar, you're not alone — and you're not unlucky. There's a specific reason recurring injuries happen, and it's almost never what you think.

Pain Going Away Is Not the Same as Being Fixed

This is the most important thing I tell new clients: pain is a signal, not the problem. When the signal stops, it doesn't mean the problem is gone — it means your body has adapted enough to quiet the alarm. The underlying cause is still there.

This is why most people feel fine after a few weeks of rest, go back to their sport, and have the same thing flare up within a month. Nothing was actually fixed — the pain just went away.

The Real Culprit: Compensatory Movement Patterns

When something hurts, your body compensates. You shift your weight, change your stride, favor one side. These compensations happen automatically — you often don't even notice them. But over time, they load structures that weren't designed to carry that load. And eventually, something else breaks.

The injury you're treating is often not the original problem. It's the third or fourth thing that broke down because the first one was never properly addressed.

Why Rest Alone Doesn't Work

Rest reduces inflammation. That's useful in the acute phase. But rest doesn't retrain movement patterns. It doesn't rebuild the strength and stability that was lost. It doesn't address the mechanical issue that caused the problem in the first place.

Without structured rehabilitation that targets the actual root cause — not just the painful area — you're just waiting for the next flare-up.

What Fixing It Actually Looks Like

  • A full movement assessment — how you actually move, not just where it hurts
  • Identifying the compensation patterns that are loading the wrong structures
  • Manual therapy to restore mobility and reduce protective tension
  • Progressive strength and stability work targeting the root cause
  • A return-to-sport plan that doesn't just get you back — it gets you back better

This takes more than six sessions of generic exercises. It takes someone who knows your history, watches you move, and adjusts the plan every single week based on what they see. That's what we do.

The Bottom Line

If your injury keeps coming back, the treatment you've been doing hasn't addressed the root cause. That's not a criticism — it's how most PT is structured. But you deserve better than managing the same problem on repeat.
