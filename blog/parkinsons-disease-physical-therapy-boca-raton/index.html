Parkinson's Disease Physical Therapy in Boca Raton: Movement, Gait, and Quality of Life

By Dr. Ezra Miller, PT, DPT  ·  Empower Fitness  ·  Boca Raton, FL

Exercise is the single best-evidenced intervention for slowing Parkinson's disease progression and maintaining function. Not medication, not surgery — exercise. Physical therapy for Parkinson's disease in Boca Raton is one of the areas where in-home PT has the most significant impact on quality of daily life. I specialize in evidence-based movement therapy for Parkinson's patients throughout South Florida.

Why Exercise Is the Best Medicine for Parkinson's

Multiple high-quality studies demonstrate that aerobic exercise and resistance training slow neurodegeneration in Parkinson's disease, improve dopamine function, and reduce symptom severity. LSVT BIG (Lee Silverman Voice Treatment for motor function) is the best-evidenced PT protocol, producing large-amplitude movement training that directly combats the bradykinesia (slowness and small movements) central to Parkinson's.

Exercise benefits for Parkinson's:

  • Reduced motor symptom severity (tremor, rigidity, bradykinesia)
  • Improved gait speed, stride length, and step symmetry
  • Reduced fall frequency
  • Improved cognitive function
  • Better mood and reduced depression symptoms
  • Possible neuroprotective effects — potentially slowing disease progression

Common Parkinson's PT Goals

  • Gait training: Addressing the shuffling, festinating gait of Parkinson's with cueing strategies (visual cues, auditory rhythm, internal attentional focus), stride length training, and arm swing restoration
  • Freezing of gait: PT strategies including visual floor cues, rhythmic auditory stimulation, and cognitive strategies for managing freezing episodes
  • Balance and fall prevention: Progressive challenge to postural reflexes that deteriorate in Parkinson's — anticipatory postural control, reactive balance, dual-task training
  • Axial rigidity: Rotation exercises for trunk mobility — the axial trunk is often the most rigid part of the motor system in Parkinson's and the most limiting for daily function
  • Big amplitude movement practice: LSVT BIG training produces lasting improvements in movement amplitude across the body when performed with sufficient intensity and frequency

In-Home PT for Parkinson's: The Frequency Advantage

Parkinson's PT research consistently shows that outcomes are dose-dependent — more sessions per week produce better results. The LSVT BIG protocol calls for 4 sessions per week for 4 weeks as its standard intensive phase. This is virtually impossible with clinic-based PT for most patients. In-home PT makes it achievable.

I work with Parkinson's patients throughout their disease progression — from early-stage maintenance to later-stage functional preservation — adjusting goals and approaches as the disease evolves.

Common Questions

When should PT start for Parkinson's?

As early as possible after diagnosis — ideally immediately. Early intervention when symptoms are mild produces the best long-term outcomes. PT should continue throughout the disease course.

What is LSVT BIG?

LSVT BIG is an evidence-based PT protocol developed specifically for Parkinson's disease, using intensive, high-amplitude movement training to directly address bradykinesia. I am trained in LSVT BIG principles.

Can exercise slow Parkinson's progression?

Animal research strongly suggests yes, and human studies show clear functional benefits. The neuroprotective mechanism likely involves BDNF upregulation and dopaminergic system support.
