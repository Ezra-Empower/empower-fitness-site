Why Concierge Physical Therapy Is Worth the Premium (From a DPT Who's Seen Both Sides)

By Dr. Ezra Miller, PT, DPT  |  2026-04-07  |  Boca Raton, FL
The most common question I hear from prospective clients isn't 'Can you help me?' — it's 'Is this worth the cost?' It's a fair question. Concierge physical therapy costs more upfront than an insurance co-pay. But after 10+ years practicing in both settings, I can tell you that for the right person, the comparison isn't even close.

What You Get at a Traditional PT Clinic

The insurance-based PT model is built around volume, not outcomes. Here's what a typical clinical experience actually looks like:

  • A 45-minute appointment where you're with your actual PT for 10–15 minutes; the rest is with a PT aide doing exercises you were handed on a sheet
  • 2–4 week wait for an initial appointment after getting a physician referral
  • Shared treatment space with 4–8 other patients
  • High therapist caseloads (15–20 patients/day per therapist is common)
  • Insurance authorization battles for additional visits, even when clinically indicated
  • Generic protocols, because individualization takes time clinics don't have

None of this is a criticism of clinic therapists — most are skilled, well-intentioned clinicians forced to operate in a broken system. But the system is optimized for billing efficiency, not patient outcomes.

What You Actually Get With Concierge PT

Concierge physical therapy inverts the model entirely. At Empower Fitness:

  • 60 minutes, 100% with Dr. Miller — no aides, no handoffs, no interruptions
  • Same-week appointments — most clients are seen within 2–3 days
  • We come to you — your home, your schedule, zero commute or wait time
  • No referral needed — Florida is a direct-access state for PT
  • No authorization limits — care continues as long as it's clinically appropriate
  • Truly individualized treatment — your protocol is built around your life, not a generic template

For a busy executive in Boca Raton, the commute time alone — 10–15 hours saved over a course of treatment — has real dollar value. And the faster recovery means returning to work, sport, and life sooner.

Who Concierge PT Is (and Isn't) Right For

I'll be honest: concierge PT isn't for everyone. If you have a minor sprain and straightforward insurance coverage, a good clinic is probably fine. But if any of these apply to you, the premium is absolutely worth it:

  • You have a complex or post-surgical condition that needs individualized attention
  • You've had poor results with clinic PT in the past
  • Your time is genuinely valuable and commuting to appointments is a real cost
  • You're an active adult or athlete with performance goals beyond just "pain-free"
  • You want a DPT (not an aide) doing your treatment every single session
  • You're 55+ and want PT designed around your specific longevity and independence goals

HSA and FSA Make It More Affordable Than You Think

One financial detail most people miss: physical therapy is a qualified medical expense under IRS rules, which means you can pay for it with pre-tax HSA or FSA dollars. If you're in the 24–32% federal tax bracket, that effectively reduces the out-of-pocket cost by nearly a third.

A $300 session paid through an HSA costs you the equivalent of approximately $200–220 in after-tax income. That changes the math significantly. We accept HSA and FSA payments at Empower Fitness — just bring your card.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need a doctor's referral to start concierge PT?

No. Florida is a direct-access state for physical therapy. You can book directly with Dr. Miller without a physician referral. We'll communicate with your doctor if needed, but it's not a requirement to get started.

Q: How many sessions will I need?

It depends on your condition. Most acute soft-tissue injuries resolve in 4–8 sessions. Post-surgical cases typically take 12–20 sessions spread over 8–16 weeks. Chronic or complex conditions vary. Dr. Miller will give you a realistic timeline at your initial evaluation.

Q: Do you accept insurance?

We are a cash-based, out-of-network practice. We don't bill insurance directly, but we accept HSA and FSA payments and can provide a superbill if you want to seek out-of-network reimbursement from your insurer.

Q: Where in South Florida do you serve?

We serve clients throughout Boca Raton, Delray Beach, Pompano Beach, and Deerfield Beach. Sessions are held in your home — no clinic visit required.
