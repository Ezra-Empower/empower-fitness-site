What to Expect in Your First Session with Empower Fitness PT

By Dr. Ezra Miller, PT, DPT  ·  Empower Fitness PT  ·  Boca Raton, FL

Most of our clients have done PT before. They know the clinic experience. What they don't know is what this looks like — because it's genuinely different. Here's a walk-through of exactly what happens when you work with us, from the moment you book to the end of your first session.

Step 1: The Consult Call

Before we ever schedule a session, we get on a call — usually 15 minutes. I want to hear what's going on in your own words. How long has this been a problem? What have you tried? What does it stop you from doing? This isn't a sales call. It's me figuring out whether I can actually help you, and being honest if I don't think I'm the right fit.

If it makes sense to move forward, we'll schedule your first session at a location that works for you — your home, your club, your gym, or your office.

Step 2: We Come to You

On the day of your first session, I show up with everything I need. You don't need a table, equipment, or a specific space — just enough room to move. I arrive on time, every time. We get started immediately. No waiting.

Step 3: The Full Evaluation

The first session is longer than a standard session because we do a thorough evaluation before any treatment. This includes:

  • Full health and injury history — not just the current issue, but everything relevant. Past injuries, surgeries, how you've been treated before and what worked or didn't.
  • Movement screen — I watch how you actually move. Squat, hinge, reach, rotate. This tells me far more than an isolated range-of-motion test.
  • Manual assessment — hands-on examination of the specific area and the structures around it. Joint mobility, tissue quality, neurological sensitivity.
  • Functional testing — sport-specific movements if applicable. How do you swing? How do you cut? Where does the breakdown happen?

Step 4: We Discuss Your Plan of Care

Once I know what I'm working with, I explain exactly what I found — in plain English. Not medical jargon. I tell you what the problem actually is, why it's been persisting, and what we're going to do about it.

We discuss your goals, your schedule, and what a realistic timeline looks like. I don't give everyone the same answer. Some people need two sessions a week for a month. Some need once a week for three months. Some need intensive work up front and then monthly maintenance. The plan fits you — not a billing template.

You will leave the first session knowing exactly what's wrong, what we're doing about it, and what to expect. No vague handouts. No 'let's see how it goes.' A real plan.

Step 5: We Get to Work

After the evaluation and plan discussion, we use the remaining time for treatment. This typically includes manual therapy — hands-on joint mobilization and soft tissue work — combined with targeted corrective exercises. Everything is explained as we go. You're not just a body on a table. You're an active participant in your own recovery.

What to Wear and Bring

  • Comfortable workout clothes you can move freely in — shorts and a t-shirt work for most
  • If you have imaging (MRI reports, X-rays, surgical notes), have them available — not required, but useful
  • Nothing else. We bring everything.

What Happens After the First Session

You'll have a home exercise program — specific to what we found, not a generic printout. You'll know exactly what to do and why. I'm also reachable between sessions. If something flares up, if you have a question, if you're not sure whether to push through something — you can reach me directly. That's part of what you're getting.

Most clients notice a meaningful difference within the first two to four sessions. Not because we're doing anything magic — but because we're actually treating the problem, not managing the symptoms.
