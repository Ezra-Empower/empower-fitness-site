Best Adjustable Beds for Seniors to Improve Sleep and Mobility

By Dr. Ezra Miller, PT, DPT  ·  2025-10-06  ·  Boca Raton, FL

Highlights

  • Adjustable beds for seniors relieve pressure points, reduce nighttime discomfort, and promote circulation - making it easier for older adults to transition in and out of bed safely.

  • Choosing the best adjustable bed depends on health conditions, mobility goals, and budget.

Finding the right sleep solution becomes increasingly important with age. In this article, we explore adjustable beds for seniors, including their benefits, types and key considerations when choosing one.

You’ll also learn what doctors recommend, how Medicare may help with costs and how physical therapy can further support strength and mobility. Keep reading to discover everything you need to know before making the best choice!

What Are Adjustable Beds for Seniors?

Adjustable beds for seniors are frames that move at different angles to create customized positions. Unlike traditional beds for seniors that remain flat, these models give users more control. A motorized base can tilt the mattress, allowing the sleeper to find the perfect angle for comfort or medical needs.

Some of these systems include massage settings, USB ports, or under-bed lighting. Others focus on simple head and leg elevation. The flexibility helps reduce pressure points, making them valuable for seniors with arthritis, poor circulation, or chronic back discomfort.

Are Adjustable Beds Worth It?

Yes, adjustable beds are worth it as they combine comfort, mobility support, and health benefits. If you often face challenges like acid reflux, snoring, or leg swelling, these bases can easily raise the head to improve breathing or elevate the legs to reduce swelling.

While these ergonomic beds may cost more than traditional frames, they provide lasting value. Comfort improvements reduce the need for extra pillows or complicated setups. This way, you can also gain independence, since many beds come with wireless remotes or smartphone apps.

Families often see them as a long-term investment. Instead of spending on multiple sleep accessories, one adjustable bed can solve several problems at once. This makes them not only practical but also cost-effective in the long run.

Are There Health Benefits to Using an Adjustable Bed?

Yes, there are many benefits of using an adjustable bed if you are a senior! Most are medically recognized and provide day-to-day improvements in quality of life:

  1. Improved circulation: Elevating the legs helps reduce swelling in the feet and ankles.

  2. Reduced snoring and sleep apnea: Raising the head opens airways and promotes better breathing.

  3. Back pain relief: Adjustable angles relieve spinal pressure and muscle tension.

  4. Better digestion: Sleeping slightly inclined minimizes acid reflux and heartburn.

  5. Enhanced independence: Seniors can change positions without relying on others.

  6. Joint pressure relief: Custom settings lower stress on hips and knees.

  7. Post-surgery comfort: Adjustable support aids recovery and prevents strain.

Do Doctors Recommend Adjustable Beds for Seniors?

Yes, doctors do recommend adjustable beds for seniors in specific situations. Specialists often suggest them for patients dealing with arthritis, circulation issues, or respiratory conditions. The ability to elevate the body makes breathing easier and reduces the risk of pressure ulcers.

That said, not every senior needs one. Doctors usually base recommendations on health status, mobility level, and long-term care goals. Seniors should consult their physician before making a purchase to ensure it fits their medical needs.

What Are The Most Common Types of Adjustable Beds for Seniors?

1- Standard Adjustable Bed

A basic model that lifts the head and feet independently. Affordable and practical, it is ideal for seniors who want comfort improvements without advanced extras. Its straightforward design makes it easy to use, and the adjustable positions help reduce pressure on joints, improve circulation, and support more restful sleep.

If you are looking to enhance comfort and mobility without added bells and whistles, this model offers a reliable and accessible solution.

2- Split Adjustable Bed

This option lets each side move independently. It’s perfect for couples, as one person can sit up or elevate their legs while the other remains flat. The ability to personalize positions on each side makes it an excellent choice for shared rooms, ensuring both sleepers enjoy optimal support. 

Beyond comfort, the independent adjustments can help reduce disturbances from movement, improve circulation, and support better rest for both users.

3- Adjustable Bed with Massage Features

Equipped with vibration or rolling massage functions, these beds go beyond basic comfort to actively reduce stiffness and improve circulation. They are especially beneficial for those dealing with chronic pain, restless legs, or muscle tension, providing gentle relief throughout the night. 

Combining adjustability with therapeutic features, these beds offer a premium solution for both comfort and wellness.

Things to Consider When Shopping for the Best Adjustable Bed

When buying your adjustable bed, use the following checklist before you confirm your purchase:

  1. Mattress compatibility: Some mattresses, such as memory foam, work better than innerspring.

  2. Size options: Twin, queen, and king sizes affect both space and cost.

  3. Ease of use: Remote controls should be simple and intuitive.

  4. Weight capacity: Stronger frames last longer and support more weight.

  5. Noise level: Quiet motors prevent nighttime disruptions.

  6. Durability: Look for steel frames and high-quality mechanisms.

  7. Warranty coverage: Extended protection is valuable for motors and electronics.

  8. Budget range: Costs vary widely, from basic to luxury models.

Do I Need a Special Frame for an Adjustable Bed?

No, you don’t need a special frame for an adjustable bed as most designs fit standard bedroom furniture. However, dedicated frames often maximize performance and durability.

Can Medicare Cover the Cost of Adjustable Beds?

Yes, Medicare can cover the cost of adjustable beds if they are classified as durable medical equipment (DME) and prescribed by a doctor. Coverage typically applies to hospital-style beds that meet specific medical needs.

However, luxury adjustable beds with features like massage or smart technology are usually not covered, meaning seniors would need to pay out of pocket. In some cases, partial coverage may still be available if the bed is directly tied to a documented medical condition.

FAQs About Adjustable Beds for Seniors

Can I Use a Regular Mattress on an Adjustable Bed?

Yes, you can use a regular mattress on an adjustable bed, but only if it is flexible enough to bend. Memory foam and latex usually work well, while traditional spring mattresses may not adapt properly.

Do Adjustable Beds Need Special Sheets?

Yes, adjustable beds need special sheets with deeper pockets or elastic corners. This ensures the fabric stays in place when the bed moves into different positions.

What Is the Life Expectancy of an Adjustable Bed?

The life expectancy of an adjustable bed is usually between 7 and 10 years. Longevity depends on usage, motor quality, and regular maintenance.

What’s the Difference Between Hospital Beds and Adjustable Beds for Seniors?

The difference between hospital beds and adjustable beds for seniors is that hospital beds are medical-grade, with safety rails and mobility support, while adjustable beds focus on comfort features like head and foot elevation.

Who Should Not Use Adjustable Beds?

Seniors who need side rails to prevent falls, patients recovering from major surgery, or those with advanced conditions like severe paralysis or late-stage dementia should not use adjustable beds. These cases usually require a hospital bed.
