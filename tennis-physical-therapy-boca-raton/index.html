Why It Keeps Coming Back

Playing through the pain
is not a treatment plan.

Tennis places unique repetitive demands on the shoulder, elbow, wrist, and knee. The serve, the backhand, the lateral movement — every shot is a potential injury vector when mechanics break down or volume increases. Most players manage. They ice it, modify their grip, play fewer sets. The injury keeps coming back.

  • Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis) from racket impact and stroke mechanics
  • Shoulder instability and anterior translation from serving
  • Wrist and forearm overuse injuries from grip and impact
  • Knee pain and IT band syndrome from lateral movement
  • Calf and Achilles issues from explosive court movement
