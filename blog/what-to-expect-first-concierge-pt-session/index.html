What to Expect in Your First Concierge PT Session

By Dr. Ezra Miller, DPT  ·  Empower Fitness PT  ·  Boca Raton, FL

Most people who reach out to us have never done concierge PT before. They know the clinic experience — waiting rooms, hot packs, handouts — but not this. Here's exactly what to expect when we show up at your door.

We Come to You

The session happens wherever works for you — your home, your building gym, your club, your office. We bring everything we need. You don't need any equipment, and you don't need to drive anywhere or sit in a waiting room. Just be ready at the time we agreed on.

The First 15 Minutes: We Listen and Assess

The first session starts with a conversation. I want to understand what's been going on — not just the current injury, but the history. How long has this been a problem? What have you tried? What makes it better or worse? What do you actually want to be able to do that you can't do right now?

Then comes a movement assessment. I watch how you actually move — not just the isolated painful area, but how your whole body is loading and compensating. This is where I usually find the real issue, which is often not exactly where the pain is.

The Next 40 Minutes: Hands-On Work

Based on what I find, we get to work. This typically includes some combination of:

  • Manual therapy — hands-on joint and soft tissue work to restore mobility and reduce protective tension
  • Targeted exercise — specific movements designed to address what we found, not a generic printout
  • Movement coaching — correcting the patterns that are loading the wrong structures
  • Education — I explain what I'm finding and why, so you understand what's actually happening in your body

The entire hour is with me — Dr. Ezra. No aide, no assistant, no switching rooms. If something isn't working, I adjust immediately. That kind of responsiveness is only possible when one person is fully focused on you the entire time.

The Last 5 Minutes: Your Plan

At the end of the session I'll tell you exactly what I found, what we're going to do about it, and what I want you to do between now and the next session. You'll leave with a clear picture — not a vague handout with generic exercises.

What to Wear and Bring

  • Comfortable workout clothes you can move freely in
  • Nothing else — we bring everything we need
  • If you have imaging (MRI, X-ray reports), feel free to have them available but it's not required

One More Thing

A lot of clients tell me after the first session that they felt more assessed and more understood in that one hour than in months of clinic PT. That's not because I'm special — it's because the model allows it. Full hour, one doctor, completely focused on you. That's the difference.
