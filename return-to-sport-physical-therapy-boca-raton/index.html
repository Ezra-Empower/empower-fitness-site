Frequently Asked Questions

Q: When can I start return-to-sport training after my injury?
Return-to-sport training begins when you're pain-free and have demonstrated adequate strength and range of motion. This typically occurs 3-6 weeks post-injury depending on severity. We don't rush; we build progressively.
Q: How is return-to-sport PT different from regular physical therapy?
Regular PT addresses pain and basic function. Return-to-sport PT adds sport-specific demands: high-speed movements, directional changes, explosive power, and competitive scenarios. We train the exact demands of your sport.
Q: How long until I can return to full competition?
Typical return-to-sport timelines are 10-14 weeks from injury, depending on injury type and your starting fitness level. Some athletes progress faster; some need more time. We don't clear you until you've passed all performance tests.
Q: What if I've already done standard PT and still don't feel ready?
Many athletes plateau after standard PT. Our return-to-sport program specifically addresses strength gaps and sport-specific conditioning that traditional PT doesn't target. We'll assess where you are and pick up where standard PT left off.
Q: Does return-to-sport training help prevent re-injury?
Yes. By progressively exposing your body to sport-specific demands and building comprehensive strength, we reduce re-injury risk significantly. Studies show athletes who complete proper return-to-sport programs have 50%+ lower re-injury rates.
